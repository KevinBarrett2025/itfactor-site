Guide 03

Self-tape checklist for actors

A clean checklist protects your tape from avoidable mistakes. When you are tired or rushing to deliver, structure matters. This is the fast pass worth running before every submission.

Checklist Submission prep Export quality

Before you record

  • Confirm the requested framing, file type, and slate requirements
  • Set your background, light, and reader position
  • Check audio before you start the first full take
  • Make sure wardrobe supports the scene without distracting from it

While you record

  • Keep the camera stable and the eyeline consistent
  • Reset the performance instead of pushing through major technical issues
  • Mark strong takes while the session is still fresh
  • Capture the slate in the exact format casting requested

Before you export

  • Watch the selected take once for performance and once for technical quality
  • Confirm that the trim starts and ends cleanly
  • Use professional file naming instead of vague draft labels
  • Choose compression settings that balance clarity with manageable size

Before you send

  • Open the final export and check that it plays correctly
  • Verify the slate, scene label, and any requested details
  • Double-check the delivery destination and instructions
  • Send the version that feels strongest, not just the last one recorded
Why this matters

Actors often lose polish in the last five minutes of the process. The checklist is there to protect the send, not to slow it down.
