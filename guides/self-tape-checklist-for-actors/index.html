Quick Answer

What this page covers.

This checklist is designed for the last stretch of the process, when you need to confirm that the tape looks right, is named cleanly, and is ready to send.

Before recording

Confirm the ask, the framing, and the room setup so you are not fixing basics later.

Before export

Check the selected take, the trim, and the file name so the finished tape feels professional.

Before sending

Make sure the final file opens cleanly and matches the actual delivery instructions.

Before you record

  • Confirm the requested framing, file type, and slate requirements
  • Set your background, light, and reader position
  • Check audio before you start the first full take
  • Make sure wardrobe supports the scene without distracting from it

While you record

  • Keep the camera stable and the eyeline consistent
  • Reset the performance instead of pushing through major technical issues
  • Mark strong takes while the session is still fresh
  • Capture the slate in the exact format casting requested

Before you export

  • Watch the selected take once for performance and once for technical quality
  • Confirm that the trim starts and ends cleanly
  • Use professional file naming instead of vague draft labels
  • Choose compression settings that balance clarity with manageable size

Before you send

  • Open the final export and check that it plays correctly
  • Verify the slate, scene label, and any requested details
  • Double-check the delivery destination and instructions
  • Send the version that feels strongest, not just the last one recorded
Why this matters

Actors often lose polish in the last five minutes of the process. The checklist is there to protect the send, not to slow it down.

FAQ

Quick answers before you send.

Why use a checklist if the tape already feels done?
Because the last five minutes are where rushed details often slip through. A fast checklist protects the send.
Should I check performance and technical quality separately?
Yes. One pass helps you judge the work, and another helps you catch things like trim, sound, and playback issues.
Do I need to over-check every tiny detail?
No. Keep it short and practical. The goal is to catch the obvious misses, not create new stress before delivery.
